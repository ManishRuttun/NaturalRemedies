Natural remedies for everyday illnesses

Tea – teas containing components like licorice and slippery elm coat the throat, reducing discomfort.

Honey – sore throats and coughs can be relieved by its throat-coating characteristics. Serve with a spoon or stir into tea.

Ginger – aid in the relief of nasal congestion and other respiratory difficulties caused by the common cold or allergies to the environment.

Cinnamon – loaded with antioxidants and has potent anti-inflammatory properties.

Lemon – Immune System Support. One lemon can offer about half of your recommended daily value of vitamin C.

Breathe in and breathe out: The calming effects of deep breathing

Deep breathing is beneficial to our health and can aid in the transformation of negative thoughts into more positive ones. To put it another way, we can learn to use our bodies and breath to make our minds feel better.

You may notice that your breathing becomes shallow and rapid when you are anxious, scared, afraid, worried, or furious. When you're unhappy, your breath will originate from higher up in your chest. Your breathing will be slower and deeper during tranquil moments. Your stomach and beneath your ribs will be the source of your breath.
