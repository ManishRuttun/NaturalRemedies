Types of dermatitis and how to treat them

Autumn is traditionally known for its sweater weather, peak leaf gazing, and pumpkin-spiced everything. Because of the cool, dry air and pollen, it's also a season for skin flare-ups for those with dermatitis.

It's crucial to feel at ease in your own skin, but dermatitis can have a negative impact on your self-esteem. Fortunately, there are techniques to cure common symptoms that affect your skin and quality of life in a controllable way.

This article explains what dermatitis is and how to manage it, giving you the assurance you need to be your best self.

What is dermatitis?

Dermatitis is a skin disorder that causes itching, dryness, flaking, and cracking. Contact dermatitis, atopic dermatitis, and seborrheic dermatitis are all common types of dermatitis.

What is contact dermatitis?

Have you ever had skin irritation as a result of trying a new soap, cosmetic, or cleaning product? If so, you've experienced contact dermatitis, which comes in two varieties: allergic and irritating.— allergic and irritant.

Allergic contact dermatitis When certain substances come into touch with your skin, they induce allergic reactions. Pollen, scents, food, poison oak or poison ivy, latex, and personal care items are all common allergies. When over-the-counter triple antibiotic ointment or Neosporin ® (bacitracin) is used for an extended period of time, it is the most prevalent cause of contact allergy.

Allergic contact dermatitis symptoms:

  • dry, itchy, flaky or cracked skin
  • a rash
  • facial swelling
  • in severe cases, blistering.

How to treat contact dermatitis:

  • Identify and avoid irritants or allergens triggering your skin reactions. Wear protective gloves when using chemicals.
  • Remove any irritants or allergens from your skin as soon as possible using warm water and soap.
  • Avoid scratching affected areas. Scratching can lead to open wounds and cause a skin infection.
  • Try over-the-counter Cortef ® (hydrocortisone) cream or ointment.

What is seborrheic dermatitis?

Seborrheic dermatitis is a chronic skin condition that primarily affects the scalp, but it can also affect other body parts.

There is no cure for seborrheic dermatitis, and no direct cause has been established. The condition is commonly caused by stress, cold weather, greasy skin, hormonal fluctuations, and a fungus called Malassezia.

Seborrheic dermatitis symptoms:

  • dry scalp
  • red skin
  • dandruff or skin flaking
  • scaly patches
  • crusted sores
  • itching or burning sensation.

How to treat seborrheic dermatitis:

Seborrheic dermatitis is no cure, however there are treatments that can help with the symptoms. Reduce the symptoms by establishing and keeping to a new regimen.

  • Avoid scratching affected areas to prevent hair loss and skin infections.
  • Wash your scalp regularly. Your health care provider may prescribe a topical solution or antifungal shampoo to treat affected areas.
  • Consider over-the-counter dandruff shampoos containing selenium sulfide, such as Head and Shoulders ® and Selsun Blue ®
