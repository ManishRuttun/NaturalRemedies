Six ways to boost your immune system naturally before you get sick

When you have a common cold, flu, or other cold-weather sickness, your immune system is your first line of defense. Your immune system aids in the healing and maintenance of your health. To function properly, your immune system requires nutritious diets, regular exercise, and a low level of stress. However, excessive inflammatory responses can lead to chronic (long-term) diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and various malignancies. So, how can you support and balance your immune system's reaction so that you can get and stay healthy?

Here are six healthy living strategies you can use to boost your immune system:

  • drink plenty of fluids
  • exercise regularly
  • limit stress
  • get plenty of sleep
  • include probiotics from foods or supplements
  • eat a colorful, well-balanced diet.

Water is essential for immune health

The best thing you can drink is still water. Water consumption varies by person, but aim for eight 8-ounce glasses each day. That can be difficult. After several hours of sleep, your body becomes dehydrated, so take a glass of water as soon as you wake up. Set reminders throughout the day or drink a glass of water before each meal if you have trouble drinking enough water. Caffeine-free hot tea can be included in your usual water intake.

Exercise regularly to strengthen immunity

Exercise is important for preventing chronic diseases such as heart disease and high blood pressure, as well as maintaining a healthy weight. Exercise also helps to keep your immune system in good shape. It promotes blood circulation, which helps your immune system work more efficiently.

Reduce stress for increased immune function

Your immune system may be overworked, and regular stress may sap your ability to stay healthy. Your immune system can be strained by daily stimuli, both great and minor. That is why it is so important to prioritize self-care. Schedule daily activities that will help you "recharge your tank." Everyone's definition of self-care is different. One example is scheduling time to read, meditate, talk a stroll, pursue a hobby, or get a massage.

Sleep, a natural immunity booster

Sleep is critical for the physical and mental well-being of your body and mind. When you don't get enough sleep, your natural immune cells and inflammatory cells both decrease. A good night's sleep is beneficial to your immune system. Adults should aim to get at least seven to eight hours of sleep per day. Children and teenagers require more sleep than adults.

Food and drink to boost your immune system

A strong immune system necessitates a nutritious diet. Plant-based, fiber-rich, protein-rich, and healthy fat-rich diets can help you feel better and heal faster. Protein is also necessary for a mending body. Probiotics, living bacteria, and yeasts, which are beneficial to your gut health and digestive system, are found in some of the finest foods to improve immunity. Fermented and cultured foods include probiotics. Look for the words "live active cultures" on the food label.

Eat colorful foods that boost your immune system

A nutritious, well-balanced diet can help you stay healthy. To receive a range of nutrients, eat a rainbow of veggies and fruits every day. A healthy diet also includes lean meats and complex carbs like brown rice and quinoa. Reduce your intake of processed meals, sweets, and nutrient-poor beverages like soda and alcoholic beverages.
